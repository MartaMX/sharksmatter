# 🦈 #sharksmatter
# Understanding Shark-Human Interactions to Promote Conservation and Safer Oceans

## Overview

This project is a data analysis study conducted to support an NGO focused on shark conservation. Using historical shark attack data, the goal is to identify meaningful patterns in shark-human interactions and provide evidence-based insights that can contribute to safer ocean practices, sustainable tourism, and informed conservation policies.

The analysis was performed using **Python**, **Pandas**, and **Matplotlib**, following a data cleaning, exploration, and visualization workflow.

---

## Problem Statement

Shark conservation organizations require reliable, data-driven evidence to better understand:

* Where shark-human interactions occur
* Which shark species are most commonly involved
* Which human activities carry the highest risk
* Under what circumstances attacks become fatal

Understanding these trends can help:

* Improve public safety through informed ocean-use guidelines
* Support sustainable coastal tourism
* Develop conservation policies that protect shark populations while reducing human risk

---

## Research Questions

This study evaluates the following hypotheses:

### Hypothesis 1

**Fatal shark attacks occur more frequently in provoked incidents than in unprovoked incidents.**

### Hypothesis 2

**A small number of shark species account for the majority of reported shark attacks worldwide.**

### Hypothesis 3

**Surfing is the activity most associated with shark attacks and is also the activity most likely to result in fatal attacks.**
